Phishing Attacks

Source 1: How To Recognize and Avoid Phishing Scams

URL https://consumer.ftc.gov/articles/how-recognize-and-avoid-phishing-scams
Site Author Federal Trade Commission (FTC)
Abstract This article from the FTC provides comprehensive guidance on identifying and avoiding phishing scams. It outlines the normal modus operandi of cybercriminals, for example, by sending an imitation email or an sms that seems to be genuine. The article describes the signs people should watch, for instance, links, suspicious messages asking for personal information, or using pressure language. It also reminds people to be careful when providing information almost anyone can access via the Internet, provides instructions for reporting phishing scams, and strives to remind everyone to be aware and alert to prevent identity theft and subsequent monetary losses.

Source 2: How to protect against phishing: 18 tips for spotting a scam

URL https://us.norton.com/blog/how-to/how-to-protect-against-phishing
Site Author Clare Stouffer
Abstract Stouffer's guide on phishing prevention provides a detailed list of 18 strategies for recognizing and defending against phishing attempts. It offers simple and effective advice on reducing risks, such as checking the sender's identity, not clicking on unfamiliar links, and employing antivirus programs to prevent contaminated action. This article also addresses other advanced yet extended types of phishing: spear-phishing and vishing (voice phishing); it again emphasizes the necessity to discover more about such types. Through the campaign, Stouffer Education and Caution wants to help users detect or avoid phishing scams that would result in scams, identity theft, and unauthorized access to secured data.
