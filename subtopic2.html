Source 1: Understanding Ransomware Threat Actors: LockBit

URL https://www.cisa.gov/news-events/cybersecurity-advisories/aa23-165a
Site Author Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency (CISA)
Abstract The lockBit ransomware group is described in detail by CISA's advisory to understand their operational activity and objectives better. The document outlines how LockBit operates, how the ransomware group delivers it, how they leverage superior encryption tools, and how they compel victims to pay them because they threaten to release the stolen information. The framework offers practical advice to organizations and offers a set of security recommendations to guard against attacks, identify early caution indicators, and react promptly to threats. The advisory focuses on the necessity of an organizational and comprehensive approach as the most effective way to prevent oneself from becoming a victim of LockBit and other ransomware.

Source 2: New Qilin ransomware encryptor features stronger encryption, evasion

URL https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/new-qilin-ransomware-encryptor-features-stronger-encryption-evasion/
Site Author Bill Toulas
Abstract This article illustrates that Qilin ransomware has recently surfaced in the market, referred to as 'Qilin.B,' and has made important changes in the encryption methodology and the strategies for evasion, enhancing the problems connected with the identification and counteraction. Toulas describes how these advancements in ransomware design make decryption more difficult and enable the assailants to stay in the network longer. From the article, readers can also get a technical understanding of what Qilin.B is capable of and understand how information security teams can counter it. The article emphasizes the need to monitor the development of new ransomware versions to prevent more diverse cyber threats.
